Code Of Conduct

Statement

DSC MQ is dedicated to providing an inclusive and harassment-free experience for participants at all of our events. We are working events intended for networking and collaboration in the community. They exist to encourage the open exchange of ideas and expression and require an environment that recognizes the inherent worth of every person and group. While at DSC MQ or related social events, any participants, including members, speakers, attendees, volunteers, sponsors, exhibitors, booth staff and anyone else, should not engage in harassment in any form.

Photography is encouraged, but other participants must be given a reasonable chance to opt out from being photographed. If they object to the taking of their photograph, comply with their request. It is inappropriate to take photographs in contexts where people have a reasonable expectation of privacy (in bathrooms or where participants are sleeping).

Participants asked to stop any harassing behavior are expected to comply immediately. As this is an event, we like to explicitly note that the hacks created at our hackathons are equally subject to the anti-harassment policy.

In summary, be nice to each other!! üòÜ

Expected Behaviour

All event participants are expected to behave in accordance with professional standards, with this code of conduct, as well as the respective university policies at Macquarie University, and applicable laws in your state.

Get In Touch

If you feel uncomfortable or think there may be a potential violation of the code of conduct, please report it to any volunteer present either personally or at the event or contact myself personally (DSC Lead - Beau Williams) at beau.williams@icloud.com. You have the right to remain anonymous.
